Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace and a royal residence. It is located in Mysore, Karnataka, India. It used to be the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace is in the centre of Mysore, and faces the Chamundi Hills eastward.

Hampi, located in central Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site known as the "City of Ruins." It was the 14th-century capital of the prosperous Vijayanagara Empire. Set against a surreal landscape of Tungabhadra River banks and giant boulders, it features over 1,600 surviving, magnificent remnants of Dravidian temples

Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, commissioned in 1632 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Completed around 1653, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a symbol of eternal love.

Golden Temple, or Sri Harmandir Sahib, located in Amritsar, Punjab, is the holiest shrine in Sikhism, renowned for its stunning 24-karat gold-plated structure and serene Sarovar (pool of nectar). Completed in 1604, it is a global symbol of equality.

Kashmir is a mountainous region in the northwestern Indian subcontinent, known for its immense natural beauty, including the Himalayas and Dal Lake. It is a heavily disputed territory administered in parts by India (Jammu & Kashmir and Ladakh), Pakistan (Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan)

India Gate, a 42-meter-high war memorial in New Delhi, stands as a prominent landmark dedicated to over 70,000 Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Afghan Wars. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens and completed in 1931, it features the names of 13,500 soldiers inscribed on its walls.

Ladakh, established as a Union Territory on October 31, 2019, is a high-altitude, cold desert in northern India bordered by China, Pakistan, and Himachal Pradesh. Known as "Little Tibet" it features rugged, mountainous landscapes (spanning the Himalayas and Karakoram) and is famous for Buddhist monasteries.

Qutb Minar, also spelled Qutub Minar and Qutab Minar, is a minaret and victory tower, built during the Delhi sultanate, and comprising the Qutb complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Mehrauli, South Delhi, India.[3][4] It was mostly built between 1199 and 1220, contains 399 steps.
